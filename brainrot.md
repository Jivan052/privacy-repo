# Privacy Policy — Brainrot: Mindful Auto-Scroll

**Last updated:** 2026-07-05

Brainrot is a local-first browser extension. This policy explains, in full, what
data it touches and what it does with it.

## Summary

- No accounts, no sign-in, no server.
- No network requests are made by this extension, ever.
- All data stays in `chrome.storage.local` on your device.
- Uninstalling the extension deletes all of its data.
- Nothing is sold, shared, or transmitted to the developer or any third party.

## What data is collected

Everything below is generated by your own use of the extension and stored
**only** in your browser's local extension storage (`chrome.storage.local`),
which is not synced to any Google account and not accessible to websites or
other extensions.

| Data | What it is | Why it's needed |
|---|---|---|
| Auto-scroll state | Whether a session is running, paused, or stopped | Lets the side panel and content script agree on what the extension should be doing |
| Session data | Start time, videos watched, watch time, per-platform counts, lowest energy reached | Powers the live Brain Energy meter and end-of-session stats |
| Daily rollups | Per-day totals: videos watched, watch time, session count/duration, per-platform breakdown, representative Brain Energy | Powers the "today" counters and the brain-state calendar (heatmap) |
| All-time totals | Cumulative videos watched, watch time, longest session, average session length | Powers the "all time" counter |
| Theme preference | light / dark / system | Remembers your display preference |

None of this data includes your name, email, IP address, Instagram/YouTube
account identity, video content, comments, messages, or any other personally
identifiable or sensitive information. Video "identity" (used internally to
detect when one Reel/Short ends and the next begins) is a URL path or a
transient video `src`, discarded once the extension moves to the next video —
it is never stored.

## What the extension does NOT do

- It does not make any network requests — no analytics, no telemetry, no
  crash reporting, no ad SDKs, no remote config, no remote code.
- It does not read, store, or transmit the content of your Reels/Shorts,
  your Instagram or YouTube account information, your browsing history
  outside of instagram.com/youtube.com, or any personal communications.
- It does not use `chrome.storage.sync`, so your data does not leave this
  specific browser profile/device.
- It does not share data with the developer, advertisers, analytics
  providers, or any other party, because no such data ever leaves your
  device in the first place.

## Permissions and why they're needed

| Permission | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `storage` | Save session state, daily/all-time stats, and theme preference locally |
| `sidePanel` | Show the extension's UI (controls, energy meter, calendar) as a side panel |
| `alarms` | Periodically check whether an active session has gone idle for 30+ minutes so it can be auto-stopped and saved |
| Host access to `instagram.com` and `youtube.com` | Run the content script that detects real video-end events and triggers the platform's own "next video" action. No other websites are accessed. |

## Data retention and deletion

All data is retained only as long as `chrome.storage.local` holds it for this
extension:

- **Uninstalling the extension** permanently deletes all of its stored data.
- **Manually clearing it**: open the extension's service worker console
  (`chrome://extensions` → Brainrot → *service worker*) and run
  `chrome.storage.local.clear()`.
- There is no remote copy anywhere to also delete, because none is ever
  created.

## Children's privacy

The extension collects no personal information from anyone, including
children, for the reasons above.

## Changes to this policy

If the extension's data practices change in a future version, this document
